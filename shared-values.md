> We, the members of the Terry Lab, are committed to fostering an open and inclusive environment built on trust in which to learn, share, work, and grow together.

We celebrate diversity in the broadest sense of the word, recognizing that different experiences, perspectives, talents, and knowledge inspire each of us to grow as people — which in turn leads to better science and stronger community. When we elevate others, we all rise.

Members of the Terry Lab commit to treating all colleagues with dignity and respect at all times: in person, online, in the lab, outside the lab, and in the far-flung places where our fieldwork takes us. We value honesty, curiosity, open communication, and the sharing of voice across all levels of experience.

## Diversity &amp; Representation

We recognize that STEM lacks adequate diversity representation, and we actively work to change that. As a lab, we commit to:

- **Acknowledging** the cultural history and significance of the collections we use and the places we work, in our publications and public communication.
- **Recruiting and mentoring** a diverse team of scientists at all career stages.
- **Improving field safety and accessibility for all** through participation in initiatives like [FieldSafe](https://fieldsafety.net/).
- **Engaging youth** from underrepresented communities through the [PelletMap Project](/outreach/) and other outreach programs.
- **Challenging stereotypes** about who belongs in science through our teaching, mentoring, and public engagement.

## Work–Life Balance and Mental Health

We acknowledge the importance of work–life balance and mental health. Graduate school and research careers can be demanding, and we work together to ensure that our lab environment is one where people feel supported — not just professionally, but as whole human beings.

*These values are living commitments. We revisit and refine them together as our community grows.*
